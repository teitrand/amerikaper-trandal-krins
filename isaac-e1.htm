~ History of Isaac Erickson ~
by Henry Erickson

I året 1866 reiste Isaac Erickson frå heimstadet Troms i Noreg bort til sambandstatene. Ein måtte rekne med 13 vekers reisetid frå Troms til Quebec i Kanada på den tidens vanlege seilskute.

- - - - -
- - - - -



     In the year 1866 Isaac Erickson left his native Troms, Norway home and journeyed to the United States. This voyage required thirteen weeks to travel from Troms, to Quebec, Canada on the then common sailship. Some days the ship was farther back on its course in the evening than it had been that morning due to strong head-winds. As was customary all passengers were required to provide themselves with the necessary food they [needed] to consume during the entire time they were on the vessel.

     Being a true pioneer through heritage, he answered the call of the frontier and made his home in Filmore County. Two years later his desire for independence became greater and he came to Minnesota Falls Township, then Redwood County. He spent some time in Old Yellow Medicine City or Upper Agency as a gunsmith and also carried mail on foot in the summertime and in the winter time on skis, between Upper Agency and Redwood. He accepted the offer of the Government and purchased the SE 1/4 in Section 36 for the sum of $200.oo. He built his log cabin on the bank of the Yellow Medicine River and began the arduous task of breaking the fertile soil of his newly acquired land. He worked hard and long and his efforts were rewarded.

     In the year 1870 he was united in marriage to Ingaborg Johnson. His bride had come to this region with the Homme and Johnson Families. ...
