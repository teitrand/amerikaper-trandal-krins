~ TRANDALSONGEN ~
Trandal's song

1953 -
Arnie "Skiffle-Joe" Norse skreiv all tekst og musikk til "Songen om Trandal" men, som han sjølv har skrivet: "Trandal-sangen ble til ved meget god hjelp av JEPMUND TRANDAL."   BIRGER WIIK skreiv notater til piano, og eige forlag K.A.J. "PRINTER" © 1953 på Trandal fekk Sunnmørsposten i Ålesund å trykke opp den vakre notehefte med foto av Trandalshatten midt på framside. Prisen i bokhandel denne gong var kr. 2,50.
     Arnie "Skiffle-Joe" Norse wrote all the text and music to "The Song About Trandal" but, as he himself has written: "Trandal-sangen was conceived with plenty of good help from Jetmund Trandal."   Birger Wiik wrote the piano notes, and it was self-published under the name of K.A.J. "Printer" © 1953 on Trandal. The newspaper "Sunnmørsposten" in Aalesund printed up the attractive note-folder with a photo of Trandalshatten on the front. The price at bookstores, at the time, was 2-1/2 kroner (or about US 35¢).

"Bygdesangen er tilegnet min mor Trandal-Anna" skriver Arnie, "og all min slekt på Trandal... og barna der som endelig har fått sin egen bygdesang!".....
     "Trandal's song is dedicated to my mother, Trandal-Anna," writes Arnie, "and all my relatives on Trandal... and the children there, who finally have their very own (bygde-) song to sing"......

Ca. 1994-? - 2000-?
Dikter, komponist, nattklubb-, TV-, sirkus-, ..og "verdensstjerne på stambula Tranen" i Oslo, Arnie "Skiffle-Joe" Norse gav ut LP-kassett Nr. 12 med 16 nya glalåter, deriblant "Trandal-sangen." Den er sunget inn av "Arnie" ilag med sine to nya freske "Angels" korjenter som heiter Trine Løberg Bjørnsen (ei blonde karateekspert) som jobber i Sagafilm, og den mørke skjønnheten Elisabeth Harlem som studerer jus (og er i slekt med tidl. statsministeren.)
     Poet-writer, composer. nightclub, TV, circus performer, ..and a "World Class Star at his favorite hangout, Tranen pub" in Oslo, Arnie "Skiffle-Joe" Norse cut LP-cassette No. 12 with 16 new songs, among these "The Song About Trandal." It's sung by "Arnie" together with two of his new fresh "Angels" back-up singers: Trine Løberg Bjørnsen (a blonde karate expert) that works for Sagafilm, and a dark beauty named Elisabeth Harlem that's studying law (and is related to the ex-Prime Minister.).....

Den opprinneleg "Songen om Trandal" har 9 vers. Sanghefte med alle tekstene frå LP-kassett Nr. 12 har berre 5 vers, pluss refrenget er litt annleis enn originalen. Herunder er alle 9 opprinneleg vers, men med nya refrenget...
     The original "Song About Trandal" has 9 verses. The written text included with LP-Cassette No. 12 only has 5 verses, plus the refrain is slightly different than the original. Here's all 9 original verses, but with the new refrain....

     "The Song About Trandal" (Trandalsangen or Trandal Songen) tells the story of the Trandalers, an isolated Norse tribespeople living within Hjørundfjord in Southern Møre, ..the region of the rugged Norwegian Alps near the coastal town of Ålesund.

English summary of verses by Per P. Hauge...

     For those who don't know of Trandal, and where it's hidden: It's a place once experienced, you'll never forget!   Look in between the high alpine mountains, with their sheer cliffs falling straight down into the blue depths of Hjørundfjord. Look behind the white curtain of eternal Winter snow, where avalanches reign supreme: That's where you'll find Trandal.

     Climb up, ..up to the tops of the mountains. You'll see far and wide. Look off to Sykkylven, ..and look over to neighboring Standal across the fjord, then look straight up.. you'll be close enough to see St. Peter standing at his Gate. But down there, down where the wild white rushing river roars, down there's where you'll find Trandal: Home sweet home!

     Surrounding Trandal stands a mighty Stone Wall of Great Mountains:   "Jura" and "Raudemolen." ..and, over there is "Råna," a welcome landmark for distant ships. Closeby is "Mora-dalen" of saga fame. But, proudest mountain of all is "Trandalshatten" showing off its ancient yet everchanging new hat...

     From the time of the sagas, from times of great warriors and knights, Trandal has been there, and fought many a battle for Norway the Fatherland. But the battle was also a fight for survival against floods of hopelessness. Stone was turned into fertile soil because of hard work, great courage, and blood...

     In the Nes mountains a very rare tree grows. This tree remembers back to saga times, back when it too took part in Viking battles. It remembers the war at Hjørungavåg when it's wood supplied strong bows. Bows that shot deadly stiff arrows, ending many a young warrior's dreams...

     A thousand years of toil around large rocks and in cold stony earth fed the Trandal people and their critters too, for all those thousand years. None need starve here. They can be proud they never need ask for help either! Their battle against both mountains, ice and snow were turned into bread!....

     Off to the Arctic Ocean, the first to sail each season. Greenland's hunting-ground was their goal. None at home need starve. And, on many a freezing stormy night in an open boat they sat and bailed water and fought the waves. And, sad to say, on such stormy nights, many drowned....

     Over Time's Scheme of things, you could find many stories to tell, but the moral of THIS story is: Trandalers became well-off only because they set their hearts on winning their own battles. When their horses were taken from them (by foreign occupiers) they became their own horses, and then carried their crops on their own backs. They built their own electric power station. They built their own road. They will not wait for help that never comes.....

     In area Trandal isn't very big. Actually, it's rather small. But, the people here ...in spite of hardships and unending toil from daylight's start, are all proud to be Trandalers.   In spite of the eternal snow, avalanches, and the hardships:   This is Trandal, our home.....
